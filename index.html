Overview

Kathmandu, the capital city of Nepal, is a vibrant and culturally rich destination nestled in the heart of the Kathmandu Valley.
Surrounded by the majestic Himalayas, this city serves as the political, economic, and cultural hub of the country.
It is a melting pot of ancient traditions and modern influences, offering visitors an unforgettable experience.
Kathmandu is a destination that enchants travelers with its spiritual atmosphere, stunning heritage, and the warmth of its people, making it a must-visit for anyone exploring South Asia.

Attractions

Kathmandu Durbar Square

Kathmandu Durbar Square, also known as Hanuman Dhoka Durbar Square, is one of the most iconic and historically significant landmarks in Kathmandu, Nepal.
Situated at the heart of the city, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is a dazzling showcase of Newar architecture, ancient temples, and cultural heritage.

Boudhanath Stupa

Boudhanath Stupa, often called Boudha, is one of the largest and holiest stupas in the world, standing majestically in the northeastern outskirts of Kathmandu, Nepal.
Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is a hub of spirituality, culture, and architectural brilliance that draws pilgrims and travelers from across the globe. This ancient monument serves as a spiritual refuge and a focal point of Tibetan Buddhist culture in Nepa

Pasupatinath Temple

Pashupatinath Temple, located on the banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu, Nepal, is one of the holiest Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. With its stunning pagoda-style architecture and sacred Shiva Lingam,
it is a place of profound spiritual significance, believed to offer liberation and purification to devotees. The temple complex includes smaller shrines, cremation ghats, and courtyards where daily rituals
and festivals like Maha Shivaratri are celebrated with great fervor. Dating back to the 4th century CE, Pashupatinath has become a symbol of Nepal’s rich religious heritage and a pilgrimage destination for millions, embodying the eternal cycle of life and death while offering a serene and sacred atmosphere for reflection and worship.

  • Hotel Yak & Yeti: Hotel Yak & Yeti is a five-star luxury hotel located in the heart of Kathmandu.Which is located in Durbar Marg, Kathmandu.

  • The Dwarika’s Hotel: The Dwarika’s Hotel is a luxurious heritage hotel known for its traditional Newar architecture and design, which is located in Battisputali, Kathmandu.
    The hotel is built using ancient wood carvings and architecture, providing a truly cultural experience. It offers a tranquil escape with beautiful gardens, a spa, and
    exquisite dining options. The Dwarika’s is located slightly away from the hustle and bustle of the city, making it ideal for those seeking peace and luxury in a cultural setting.
